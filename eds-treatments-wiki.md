# EDS & Hypermobility Treatments

> Last researched: 2026-04-07
> Sources: PMC/NCBI, Ehlers-Danlos Society

## Overview

**Ehlers-Danlos Syndromes (EDS)** are a group of 13 heritable connective tissue disorders. **Hypermobility Spectrum Disorders (HSD)** are related conditions causing joint hypermobility, instability, injury, and pain without meeting full EDS diagnostic criteria.

- **Prevalence**: ~1 in 500 for all EDS/HSD combined
- **hEDS/HSD account for >95%** of all cases
- **Pain prevalence**: 86-98% of patients report chronic pain

### 📢 Major Update Coming (December 2026)

For the first time since 2017, diagnostic criteria for all EDS types and HSD will be updated. New framework to be published in American Journal of Medical Genetics on **December 1, 2026**.

## Treatment Approaches

### 1. Physical Therapy & Rehabilitation

#### Neurocognitive Therapeutic Approach
Evidence-based approach combining movement recovery with pain management:

**Key Components:**
- "Felt sense" - directing bodily sensations to healthy parts first, then painful areas
- Narrative medicine integration - patient describes pain characteristics
- Proprioceptive discrimination exercises (sponges of different textures)
- Lumbopelvic rhythm recognition
- Postural symmetry training with load distribution

**Outcomes (2021 study, n=18):**
- Reduced pain (NRS, p=0.003)
- Reduced fatigue (FSS, p=0.03)
- Reduced fear of movement (Tampa scale, p=0.003)
- Reduced pain-associated disability (ODI, p=0.03)

#### Wrist Stabilization (2024 RCT)
- Exercise vs hand orthotic comparison
- Exercise group showed improvements in pain/paraesthesia symptoms
- Both HSD and hEDS patients included

### 2. Pain Management

#### Multidisciplinary Pain Rehabilitation
- **Interdisciplinary approach** shows promise (2024 Swedish study, n=406)
- Combines physical, psychological, and educational components
- Significant improvements in pain and function vs other chronic pain conditions

#### Online Pain Management - HOPE Program (2025)
**Hypermobile Online Pain managEment (HOPE)** - First evidence-based online program for hEDS/HSD:

- **Format**: 12 modules over 8 weeks, self-paced
- **Approach**: Biopsychosocial model with pain neuroscience education
- **Feasibility**: 81-91% agreed program was feasible (FIM 4.3/5)
- **Acceptability**: 62-76% found it acceptable (AIM 3.9/5)
- **Effectiveness**: Moderate effect on worst pain intensity (d=0.63), small effect on hypermobility impact (d=0.32) at 3 months

**Key Features:**
- Pain neuroscience education
- Condition-specific education
- Reflective questions for self-management planning
- Cognitive functional therapy principles

### 3. Pharmacological Considerations

#### Analgesic Challenges
- Standard pain medications often less effective
- **Opioid caution**: Common but can lead to mismanagement (2023 case reports)
- Local anesthetic allergy reported in some patients (affects procedures)

#### Perioperative Management
- Specialized anesthesia protocols recommended (2014 guidelines)
- Tissue fragility requires careful handling
- Vascular and soft tissue fragility considerations

### 4. Associated Condition management

#### Sleep Apnea
- Obstructive sleep apnea common
- Hypoglossal nerve stimulation shown effective when CPAP fails (2023 case)

#### Dysautonomia
- Often co-occurs with hEDS/HSD
- Requires separate management approach

| Type | Mechanism | Examples |
|------|-----------|----------|
| **Nociceptive** | Tissue injury | Acute subluxations, dislocations |
| **Nociplastic** | Central sensitization | Prolonged pain leading to sensitization |
| **Neuropathic** | Nerve damage | Small fibre neuropathy, entrapments |

## Clinical Recommendations

### For Patients
1. Seek EDS-aware practitioners when possible
2. Consider online programs like HOPE if in-person access limited
3. Focus on proprioceptive training and stabilization
4. Address kinesiophobia (fear of movement) - common and treatable
5. Biopsychosocial approach most effective

### For Practitioners
1. Combine education + exercise + cognitive approaches
2. Tailor rehabilitation to individual problems
3. Focus on pain perception, not just movement recovery
4. Monitor for central sensitization
5. Be aware of tissue fragility in procedures

## Research Gaps

- No cure exists; management is symptomatic
- Limited RCTs specific to hEDS/HSD populations
- Need for larger trials of multidisciplinary interventions
- Optimal PT protocols still being established
- Genetic basis of hEDS not yet identified (unlike other EDS types)
